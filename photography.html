My Photography

Photography and how it changed my perspectives

Growing up, I played with film cameras and even recieved one for a birthday present. Yes, back then there wasn't digital yet. I took some photos but quickly lost interest. I just couldn't connect to the art of photography when I was younger. I was just snapping photos with zero purpose.

Fast forward many years, and my wife buys me a film Lomography Camera one birthday. I walked around Vancouver with this retro style camera, now that digital is the standard, and I am so supprised by the images I am able to capture on the streets. I especially like the feel of the film images which possess more grain and feel like they hold more substance.

I quickly dove into film photography buying old 35mm and 120mm cameras off ebay from Japan. These cameras were as old as me and are still in prestine condition. This new hobby of mine really made me feel happy. I started to be much more observant of things happening around me and tried to capture life as it happened. Fast forward to today, and I carry a camera with me everywhere. I still own several film camaeras but also have several digital ones. Each camera I own has its own unique feeling to me, and I match the camera to my mood that day. I then procede to hit the streets and capture life.
